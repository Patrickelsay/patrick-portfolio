# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients (founders, marketing leads at local businesses), design/content hiring managers, and potential collaborators. They arrive from a reel, a referral, or a resume link — usually an evening laptop browse. Their job: figure out fast whether Patrick is the real deal across the domain they care about (content, UX, product design, or business building), see proof, and reach out.

## Product Purpose

Patrick El-Sayegh's personal portfolio. It shows an unusually wide body of work — short-form video marketing with client results, UX/UI projects, industrial design case studies, an active venture (GetExpanded), and creative side work — as one coherent identity rather than five disconnected piles. Success = a visitor lands anywhere, understands the range within seconds, dives into proof, and sends a message.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, cinematic, systems-driven. Confident and expressive (big type, motion like an edit cut) but warm and human — humor and hustle, not corporate polish. The voice makes people feel "at ease and at home" while still signalling above-and-beyond delivery.

## Anti-references

- A literal clone of nithinmwarrier.com — it's the polish/intentionality benchmark, not a template.
- Generic SaaS landing grammar: hero-metric blocks, identical card grids, eyebrow labels over every section.
- Cream/beige "editorial restraint" portfolios; anything that reads AI-default.
- Dark "hacker/terminal" developer-portfolio costume.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the color.** The shell stays chromatically quiet; reels, renders, and UI shots carry the vibrancy. One committed brand color threads both worlds.
2. **One site, many rooms.** Light shell for reading and browsing; dark rooms where media plays. Each domain gets art direction fit to its medium — cohesion comes from type, color, and voice, not identical treatment.
3. **Proof over polish.** Real analytics, testimonials, member counts, and booked calendars are first-class content, presented as designed artifacts, not screenshots dumped in.
4. **Process is a feature, not filler.** WIP material renders in a distinct process treatment (film-strip/pinboard) — behind-the-scenes is part of Patrick's story, but never confused with finals.
5. **Every landing converts.** Direct links to any project or domain page still deliver identity (voice intro) and an exit (closing CTA).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 in both rooms, visible focus states, full keyboard paths, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternates for every animation, autoplay always muted with reduced-motion suppression, meaningful alt text written in the site's voice.
